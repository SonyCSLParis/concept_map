But far more was over than that. As a strange side-effect of the Muslim conquest, the doctrinal integrity of eastern Christendom was preserved: instead of the compromises with the Vatican that might otherwise have been inevitable, the patriarchate was able to hold to its view on the issues, such as the nature of the Trinity, that had led to so much bitter argument. The Turks were besieging Vienna in 1683 and repeatedly at war with Russia or Austria in the 130 years thereafter.
