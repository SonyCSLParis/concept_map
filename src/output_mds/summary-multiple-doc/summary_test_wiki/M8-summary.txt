The convention of 1832 and 1833 The Convention of 1832 Texans Settlers followed the news of the Mexican Civil War with great interest, hoping Santa Anna Would Win because the Texan Settlers believed that a new Federalist government would respond to their needs. They also wanted Texas to be a separate Mexican state because at the time Texas was part of a bigger state called Coahuila y Tejas Ramon Musquiz, the governor of the Department of Texas, supported these concerns.
