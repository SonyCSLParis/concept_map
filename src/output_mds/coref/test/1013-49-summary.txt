How Do Montessori Schools Report Student Progress ? Student progress , however , is measured in different ways , which may include : Student Self-Evaluations : At the elementary level , students will students prepare a monthly self-evaluation of the past three month 's work : what they accomplished , what students enjoyed the most students what they found most difficult students and what they would like to students in the three months ahead . Portfolios of Student Work : In many Montessori schools , two or three times a year , teachers ( and at the elementary level , students ) and parents go through the students ' completed work and make selections for their portfolios .
