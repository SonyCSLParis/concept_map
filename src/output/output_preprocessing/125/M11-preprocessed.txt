thursday october 1 2015 first church in wildwoods capon chapel is a mid-19th century church near the town of capon bridge in the us state of west virginia
a baptist congregation began gathering at the site of the present day church as early as 1756 and has served has a place of worship ever since
it was used as a place of worship by baptists until the late 19th or early 20th century by the southern methodist episcopal church for most of the 20th century and in this century by the united methodist church
the church cemetery contains the remains of american civil war veterans from the union and the confederacy and free and enslaved african americans
capon chapel along with its cemetery was added to the national register of historic places in 2012 in recognition of its rural religious architecture representative of the potomac highlands region and for its service as a rural church in hampshire county
